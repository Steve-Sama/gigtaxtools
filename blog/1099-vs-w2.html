1099 vs W-2: Understanding Your Tax Obligations

Learn the key differences between being a 1099 independent contractor and a W-2 employee, including tax responsibilities, deductions, and how to maximize your income.

What is a 1099 Worker?

1099 workers are independent contractors who receive a 1099 form for the income they earn. They are responsible for paying their own taxes, including federal, state, and self-employment taxes. They often have more flexibility but fewer benefits.

What is a W-2 Employee?

W-2 employees have taxes withheld from each paycheck by their employer, including federal income tax, Social Security, and Medicare. Employers also contribute to Social Security and Medicare on their behalf. Employees may receive benefits like health insurance and paid time off.

Tax Differences Between 1099 and W-2

  • Tax Withholding: W-2 employees have automatic withholding; 1099 workers must calculate and pay quarterly estimated taxes.
  • Self-Employment Tax: 1099 workers pay 15.3% self-employment tax; W-2 employees pay 7.65% while employers cover the other 7.65%.
  • Deductions: 1099 workers can deduct business expenses such as mileage, home office, and supplies; W-2 employees have limited deductions.
  • Flexibility: 1099 income can vary greatly; W-2 provides consistent paychecks.

Benefits & Drawbacks

  • 1099: Flexible schedule, control over work, more deductions, but responsible for all taxes and no employee benefits.
  • W-2: Employer handles taxes, consistent income, benefits, but less control over work and limited deductions.
